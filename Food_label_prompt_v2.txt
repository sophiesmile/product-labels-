Core principle:
Return the label(s) that describe the PRODUCT IDENTITY, not the hazard, not the contaminant, and not every ingredient mentioned in the subject.

How to decide labels:
1. Use the product field as the primary evidence.
2. Use the subject as supporting evidence when the product field is incomplete, generic, multilingual, scientific, or ambiguous.
3. Use notification_type and product_category as supporting context.
4. Default to a single label because most records have one main product identity.
5. Output multiple labels only when multiple distinct components are clearly central to the product identity.
6. Do NOT force single-label output when the product name itself clearly expresses multiple core components.
7. For multiple-label output, include only labels that are explicitly central in the product name or unmistakably supported by both product and subject.

What counts as a central product component:
1. Main food item or species in the product name.
2. Named core ingredient or formulation component explicitly present in the product name.
3. Standard product-form or identity component that is central to the product, such as:
   - sausage / salami -> sausage
   - bread / flatbread -> bread
   - caviar
   - syrup
   - vinegar
   - sesame seed in sesame halva

   - corn in corn pasta

4. For food supplements involving CBD / THC / HHC / cannabinoid / hemp extract / hemp oil:
   - include "food supplement" if the product is a supplement
   - include "cannabinoid" if CBD / THC / HHC / cannabidiol / cannabinoid is clearly indicated
   - include "hemp" if hemp is explicitly indicated in product or subject

What NOT to include:
1. Do not add a label only because it appears in the subject as:
   - an undeclared allergen
   - a contaminant
   - a hazard
   - a substance found in the product
2. Do not add allergen labels unless they are clearly part of the product identity.
3. Do not add carrier / packing / medium labels automatically unless they are clearly part of the product identity in the product name.
4. Do not add extra labels just because they are plausible.

Normalization rules:
1. Normalize synonyms, translations, scientific names, lexical variants, and product forms to the closest allowed label.
2. If a fine-grained label is not allowed, map to the nearest allowed label.
3. Important normalizations:
   - chili / chilli / chili pepper -> pepper
   - black eye bean / black-eyed bean -> cowpea
   - betel nut -> other nuts
   - wild garlic -> garlic
   - drumsticks (vegetable) -> moringa
   - salami -> sausage
   - peas / pea mix -> other peas if a more specific pea label is not clearly available
   - kebab -> meat unless the species is explicitly central and clearly supported
   - chips / crisps / potato chips -> potato chips when the product is clearly a potato snack
4. If the model thinks of a label that is not allowed, replace it with the closest allowed label instead of outputting an invalid label.

RASFF-specific rules:
